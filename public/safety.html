Know When to Act

Red Flags &
When to Seek Care

Not every symptom is an emergency, but some should never be ignored. Learn to distinguish between what can wait and what needs attention now.

Important: We do not diagnose. This page provides educational guidance to help you recognize when professional evaluation is important. Always consult a qualified healthcare provider for medical concerns.

Urgent / Emergency

Red Flag Symptoms

These symptoms may indicate a condition requiring prompt or emergency medical evaluation. Do not delay seeking care.

Fever with Pelvic or Groin Pain

Fever combined with pain in the pelvic region, lower abdomen, or groin can indicate acute prostatitis or a urinary tract infection that has become systemic. This combination suggests your body is fighting an active infection that may need urgent antibiotic treatment.

Seek immediate medical attention

Inability to Urinate (Acute Urinary Retention)

If you feel the urge to urinate but physically cannot pass urine, this is acute urinary retention. It can result from severe prostate swelling, infection, or nerve issues. This condition is painful and can damage the bladder and kidneys if not treated quickly.

Seek immediate medical attention

Blood in Urine (Hematuria)

Visible blood in your urine, whether pink, red, or brown, should always be evaluated by a medical professional. While it can have benign causes like vigorous exercise, it may also indicate infection, kidney stones, or other conditions that require investigation.

Seek immediate medical attention

Severe or Sudden Testicular Pain

Sudden, severe pain in the testicles can indicate testicular torsion, which is a medical emergency requiring treatment within hours. It can also signal epididymitis or other acute infections. Never assume severe testicular pain will resolve on its own.

Seek immediate medical attention

Blood in Semen (Persistent)

A single episode of blood in semen is often benign, especially in younger men. However, if it persists over multiple occurrences or is accompanied by pain, swelling, or urinary symptoms, it warrants a medical evaluation to rule out infection, inflammation, or other conditions.

Seek immediate medical attention
Non-Emergency / Important

When to Get Evaluated

These symptoms are not emergencies but should be discussed with a healthcare provider if they persist or worsen.

Persistent Weak Urinary Stream

A noticeably weaker or slower urinary stream that persists over weeks could indicate prostate inflammation, pelvic floor tension, or urethral narrowing. It is a common early sign worth monitoring and discussing with your doctor.

Schedule a medical appointment

Frequent Nighttime Urination (Nocturia)

Regularly waking up two or more times per night to urinate can disrupt sleep quality and may suggest prostate irritation, bladder issues, or fluid balance problems. Reducing evening fluids can help, but persistent nocturia should be evaluated.

Schedule a medical appointment

Burning or Pain During Urination

Dysuria (burning or pain when urinating) lasting more than a few days often points to a urinary tract infection, prostatitis, or sexually transmitted infection. A simple urine test can usually identify the cause and guide treatment.

Schedule a medical appointment

Persistent Pelvic Floor Pain or Pressure

Chronic pain, pressure, or discomfort in the pelvic area, perineum, or between the scrotum and rectum can indicate chronic prostatitis or pelvic floor dysfunction. A pelvic floor physical therapist or urologist can help identify the source and provide treatment.

Schedule a medical appointment

Unexplained Erectile Changes

Erectile changes lasting more than two weeks that are not linked to stress, sleep deprivation, or substance use may indicate vascular, hormonal, or neurological factors. These are often highly treatable when identified early by a healthcare provider.

Schedule a medical appointment

Painful Ejaculation

Pain during or after ejaculation can be caused by prostatitis, seminal vesicle inflammation, pelvic floor muscle tension, or infection. While often treatable, it can impact quality of life and should be discussed with a healthcare professional.

Schedule a medical appointment

Lower Back Pain with Urinary Changes

Lower back pain paired with urinary frequency, urgency, or difficulty can suggest kidney involvement, prostate inflammation, or nerve-related issues. When these symptoms appear together, it is important to have both evaluated to rule out interconnected causes.

Schedule a medical appointment
Sexual Health Awareness

STIs & Prostate Health

Understanding the connection between sexually transmitted infections and long-term prostate wellness is an important part of proactive health.

The STI-Prostate Connection

Certain sexually transmitted infections can travel to the prostate gland, causing acute or chronic inflammation known as bacterial prostatitis. Even after the initial infection is treated, residual inflammation can persist for months or years if not properly addressed. This is one reason why regular STI screening matters for long-term prostate health, not just short-term symptom relief.

When to Get Tested

  • After any unprotected sexual contact with a new partner
  • If you experience burning during urination or unusual discharge
  • Annually if you have multiple sexual partners
  • Before starting a new sexual relationship
  • If a partner has been diagnosed with an STI

A Note on Stigma

STIs are common medical conditions, not moral failings. Millions of people contract STIs each year, and most are easily treatable. Getting tested is a sign of responsibility and self-respect. Your healthcare provider has seen it all before and is there to help, not judge. Taking care of your sexual health is an integral part of taking care of your prostate health.

Be Prepared

What to Tell Your Clinician

Use this checklist before your appointment to make sure you cover everything important. Check off items as you prepare.

Pre-Appointment Checklist

0 of 7 prepared
Symptom Duration When did symptoms start? How long have they lasted? Any pattern or triggers?
Severity Scale (1-10) Rate your discomfort from 1 (mild) to 10 (worst). Note if severity changes throughout the day.
Changes in Urination Pattern Frequency, urgency, stream strength, nighttime trips, hesitancy, or incomplete emptying.
Sexual Function Changes Any changes in erectile function, ejaculation, libido, or discomfort during sexual activity.
Lifestyle Factors Daily sitting time, exercise habits, stress levels, sleep quality, alcohol/caffeine intake.
Current Supplements & Medications List all supplements, vitamins, and medications you currently take, including dosages.
Family History Any family history of prostate issues, urological conditions, or related cancers (father, brothers, uncles).

Early Evaluation Is Strength, Not Weakness

Remember: Taking control of your health proactively is a sign of strength. Do not wait for symptoms to become severe. Schedule that appointment.
