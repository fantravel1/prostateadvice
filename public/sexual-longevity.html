Understanding

Why Track This?

Sexual performance is not just about sex. It is one of the body's most sensitive health indicators.

Cardiovascular Health Signal

Morning erections indicate healthy blood vessel function and cardiovascular performance. Changes in frequency are often an early warning signal worth paying attention to.

Hormonal Health Indicator

Libido trends correlate closely with testosterone levels, thyroid function, and overall hormonal balance. Tracking over time reveals patterns invisible day-to-day.

Stress and Sleep Connection

Sleep quality and stress levels have a direct, measurable impact on sexual function. This dashboard helps you see those connections clearly over time.

Performance as Health Signal

This is health monitoring framed without shame. Sexual function is a vital sign, similar to resting heart rate or sleep efficiency. Track it the same way.

Optimization

Recovery Optimization Suggestions

Evidence-informed strategies to support your body's natural recovery systems.

Sleep Optimization

  • Keep a consistent sleep and wake schedule, even on weekends, to regulate hormonal cycles
  • Reduce blue light exposure 90 minutes before bed to support melatonin production
  • Keep the bedroom cool (65-68 F / 18-20 C) for optimal testosterone production during sleep

Stress Management

  • Practice 10 minutes of daily breathwork to activate parasympathetic nervous system recovery
  • Limit stimulants after 2 PM, as cortisol elevation impairs both sleep quality and hormonal balance
  • Schedule regular non-digital downtime to allow the nervous system genuine restoration

Pelvic Health

  • Alternate pelvic floor strengthening with conscious relaxation to prevent chronic tension
  • Include hip mobility work daily, especially deep squats and hip flexor stretches, to improve circulation
  • Avoid prolonged sitting (over 60 minutes at a time) to maintain healthy pelvic blood flow

Lifestyle Balance

  • Include 150 minutes of moderate or 75 minutes of vigorous cardio weekly for cardiovascular function
  • Prioritize whole foods rich in zinc, omega-3s, and antioxidants for hormonal and vascular support
  • Limit alcohol intake, as even moderate consumption can measurably impact hormonal balance
Protocols

Nervous System Balancing Protocols

Simple, evidence-informed techniques to calm the nervous system and support recovery.

4-7-8 Breathing Technique

A clinically studied breathing pattern that activates the parasympathetic nervous system, reducing cortisol and promoting deep relaxation.

1 Inhale quietly through your nose for 4 seconds
2 Hold your breath for 7 seconds
3 Exhale completely through your mouth for 8 seconds
4 Repeat 4 cycles. Practice daily, ideally before sleep.

Progressive Muscle Relaxation

Systematically tensing and releasing muscle groups reduces overall body tension and teaches the nervous system to recognize and release held stress.

1 Tense your feet and calves firmly for 5 seconds, then release for 15 seconds
2 Progress upward: thighs, glutes, pelvic floor, abdomen, chest, arms, hands, face
3 Focus on the contrast between tension and release at each group
4 Complete the full body scan in 10-15 minutes. Practice before bed or after stress.

Cold Exposure Benefits

Brief cold exposure stimulates the vagus nerve, increases norepinephrine, and improves vascular function, all of which support sexual health markers.

1 End your daily shower with 30 seconds of cold water. Build up gradually.
2 Focus on controlled breathing during exposure. Do not hyperventilate.
3 Work up to 2 minutes over several weeks. Consistency matters more than duration.
4 Note: Avoid if you have cardiovascular conditions. Consult your physician if unsure.
