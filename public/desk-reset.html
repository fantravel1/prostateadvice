Desk Reset Protocol

Combat the pelvic damage of prolonged sitting. Quick circulation resets, hip mobility drills, and posture corrections designed for desk workers who care about their long-term health.

The Protocol

Four Pillars of the Desk Reset

Each pillar targets a different aspect of pelvic health compromised by prolonged sitting.

5-Minute Pelvic Circulation Reset 3 exercises | ~2 min

1
Deep Squat Hold Hold a deep squat for 30 seconds. Opens the pelvic floor and restores blood flow to compressed tissues.
2
Standing Pelvic Tilts 10 slow forward-and-back tilts. Activates the deep pelvic muscles and pumps circulation.
3
Kegel Squeeze Sequence 5-second hold, 5-second release, repeat 3 times. Engages and then fully relaxes the pelvic floor.

Hip Mobility Drills 4 exercises | ~2 min

1
Seated Hip Circles 10 circles each direction while seated. Mobilizes the hip joints and loosens tight connective tissue.
2
Hip Flexor Stretch (Left) Lunge position, push hips forward. Hold 30 seconds. Releases the chronically tight psoas.
3
Hip Flexor Stretch (Right) Same as above on the right side. Symmetry matters for balanced pelvic floor function.
4
Figure-4 Stretch (Both Sides) Cross ankle over opposite knee, lean forward. 30 seconds each side. Targets deep hip rotators.

Breathing Resets 3 techniques | ~1.5 min

1
Box Breathing (4-4-4-4) Inhale 4 sec, hold 4 sec, exhale 4 sec, hold 4 sec. Calms the nervous system and relaxes the pelvic floor.
2
Extended Exhale (4-4-6) Inhale 4 sec, hold 4 sec, exhale 6 sec. Longer exhale activates the parasympathetic response.
3
Diaphragmatic Belly Breath Place hands on belly. Breathe deeply so hands rise. 5 slow breaths. Directly massages pelvic organs.

Posture Micro-Corrections 4 quick fixes | ~30 sec each

1
Sit-Bone Awareness Rock side to side on your sit bones. Find the balanced center. This tilts your pelvis to its neutral, healthy position.
2
Shoulder Blade Squeeze Squeeze shoulder blades together, hold 5 sec, release. Repeat 5 times. Opens the chest and aligns the spine.
3
Chin Tuck Gently tuck chin to create a double chin, hold 5 sec, release. Reverses forward head posture that cascades into pelvic tilt.
4
Feet Flat Reset Plant feet flat, hip-width apart. Press all four corners of each foot into the floor. Reconnects your kinetic chain from ground to pelvis.
