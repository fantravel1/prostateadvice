Bridge Hold
8 reps · 10s hold with pelvic squeeze

Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat on the floor. Press through your heels to lift your hips into a bridge. At the top, squeeze your pelvic floor muscles firmly and hold for 10 seconds. Lower slowly and rest 5 seconds between reps. This integrates glute strength with pelvic floor activation.

Squat Pulse
12 reps · Bodyweight squat + pelvic engage

Stand with feet shoulder-width apart. Lower into a bodyweight squat until thighs are parallel to the floor. At the bottom of the squat, engage your pelvic floor and hold for 3 seconds, then stand back up while maintaining the engagement. Release at the top. This builds functional pelvic strength in real-world movement patterns.

Standing Hip Hinge
10 reps · Hinge + pelvic floor activation

Stand with feet hip-width apart, slight bend in knees. Hinge forward at the hips keeping your back straight, lowering your torso until roughly parallel with the floor. As you hinge, engage the pelvic floor. As you return to standing, drive through your hips and maintain the pelvic contraction. Release at the top. Diaphragmatic Breathing
5 cycles · 4-7-8 breathing pattern

Lie down or sit comfortably. Place one hand on your chest and one on your belly. Inhale deeply through your nose for 4 seconds, feeling your belly rise. Hold your breath for 7 seconds. Exhale slowly through your mouth for 8 seconds. As you exhale, consciously release all tension in your pelvic floor. This activates the parasympathetic nervous system and releases chronic pelvic tension.

Hold 45 seconds

Kneel on the floor and sit back on your heels. Fold forward with your arms extended in front of you or alongside your body. Rest your forehead on the floor and breathe deeply. With each exhale, allow your pelvic floor to soften and release. Widen your knees for a deeper stretch. This is one of the most effective poses for pelvic floor relaxation.

Reverse Kegel
10 reps · Gentle push / release

The opposite of a Kegel. Instead of contracting upward, gently push downward as if trying to release gas. The sensation is one of gentle expansion and opening. Hold the push for 3 seconds, then fully relax for 5 seconds. This exercise is critical for men who carry chronic tension in their pelvic floor and helps restore balance between strength and flexibility.

Progressive Pelvic Release
6 reps · Tense 5s / fully release 10s

Based on progressive muscle relaxation principles. Tense your entire pelvic floor firmly for 5 seconds, building to maximum contraction. Then, suddenly and completely release all tension. Spend 10 full seconds noticing the difference between tension and relaxation. Focus on the "letting go" sensation. This trains your body to recognize and release chronic pelvic holding patterns.

4-Week Plan

Weekly Progression

Follow this progressive plan to safely build pelvic floor strength and endurance over 4 weeks.

Week 1

Foundation

  • 3 sessions per week
  • Kegel Holds & Quick Flicks only
  • 5 reps per exercise
  • 1 relaxation session
  • Focus on finding the right muscles
Week 2

Building

  • 4 sessions per week
  • Add Bridge Hold & Squat Pulse
  • 8 reps per exercise
  • 2 relaxation sessions
  • Increase hold times by 2 sec
Week 3

Strengthening

  • 5 sessions per week
  • Add Hip Hinge & Wall Sit
  • Full reps as prescribed
  • 2 relaxation sessions
  • Focus on endurance under fatigue
Week 4

Integration

  • Daily practice
  • All 6 strength exercises
  • Full reps + extra set
  • Alternate strength & relaxation
  • Integrate into daily movement
